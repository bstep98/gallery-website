An Infinite Sky

This view was the first thing I thought of when I was given the theme for this photoset. I pass by this view every day on my way to class, and doing it justice was at the forefront of my mind when I photographed it.

Water Break

This piece was a bit hard to capture. I don't have a lot of experience capuring moving subjects. That being said, it took a lot of work to capture this photo, and I'm more than satisfied with the outcome.

Peeking Through the Trees

I wanted this piece to convey a sense of abandonment. I wanted the viewer to feel like they were trapped in the woods, with no idea how they got there, but I wanted there to be an underlying sense of optimism behind that.

Marking the Land

I tried to convey the strength of nature with this piece. I wanted the tree to look like it towered over everything around it, including everything made by man.

A Trunk Full of Wonder

I was hoping for this piece to feel like somewhat of a journey, like the viewer was as small as an ant, travelling up the trunk of the tree.

Two Lights

This piece should convey the power of nature, showing how what is man-made cannot compare to the sheer majesty and abundance that nature gives us. I wanted the sun almost blind the viewer, with their only refuge being the old lamppost.

Age and Rebirth

Polarity. I wanted this piece to give all who view it a sense of polarity, and how related death and birth can be, sometimes even being adjacent.

A Curious Duck 1

When I captured this piece, I ran into similar problems that I faced with Water Break. It was hard for me to pose the subject, and I wasn't very keen on getting too close.

A Curious Duck 2

Despite my subject's constant movement, I was able to capture this piece and the one prior. It was hard for me to choose between this piece and A Curious Duck 1, so I decided that both deserved to be in this gallery. I hope this duck is proud of me.

Feng Shui

No photo gallery of the Duck Pond would be complete without pictures of the water itself. I wanted this piece to convey the pure size and scope of the Duck Pond, while still showing the beauty of the campus around it.

Your Feature Film

I actually took two versions of this piece. One where the wood was in focus, and one where the water and the island were in focus. I thought that the pond itself got a good amount of the spotlight in Feng Shui, so I decided showing the beauty and framing of the dock would be a good choice.

A Break By the Water

I wanted this piece to be a marriage of Your Feature Film and Feng Shui. I wanted to find a way to balance the water and nature with something man-made, that being the wood. As a send-off for this entire gallery, I think this piece greatly shows the majesty of the nature in Blacksburg.

About me

My name is Stephen Balani! I'm a 20 year old Communication Studies major and Theatre Arts minor at Virginia Tech. I love performance! Really, I love art in any form. I love taking what I'm feeling on the inside and manifesting it into something that others can look at. I'm super excited to share my work! Thank you!
